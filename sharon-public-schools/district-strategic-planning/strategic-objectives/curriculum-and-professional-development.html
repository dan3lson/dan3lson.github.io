Curriculum and Professional Development Objectives

We start with our Strategic Outcomes in mind and then determine what actions to complete to achieve our ambitious goals.

Along the way, we monitor and publicly report our progress, as part of the district planning process, to hold ourselves accountable to the Sharon Public Schools community.

Initiatives for SY 2018-2019

Professional Development Plan

Expected Outcomes
Increase access to challenging curriculum and instruction for all students

Actions
Develop a district professional development plan that improves best practice for student- centered, culturally responsive learning

Status
Yellow

Under-represented Honor Students

Expected Outcomes
Increase the number of under-represented students enrolled in accelerated/honors/AP math/Science/English courses at the secondary level

Actions
Create a multi-tiered system of support that provides multiple ways for students to receive academic and social/emotional supports and interventions

Status
Yellow

Initiatives for SY 2019-2022

K-5 Academic Growth

Expected Outcomes
K-5 students will show growth in reading, writing and mathematics

Actions
Align curriculum vertically and horizontally

English Learners

Expected Outcomes
ACCESS scores for EL (English Learners) students will increase for the next three years and the rate of transitioning EL students to FLEP (Formerly Limited English Proficiency) students will match or exceed the state rate

Actions
Gather and utilize specific subgroup data to identify student need, creating data teams at every grade level or subject area and developing a district wide system for looking at student data

Achievement Gaps

Expected Outcomes
Decrease the achievement gaps across all subgroups

Actions
Promote inclusive practices based on shared ownership for learning and high expectations for all

Learning Opportunities

Expected Outcomes

Actions
Explore and develop project-based learning opportunities for students
