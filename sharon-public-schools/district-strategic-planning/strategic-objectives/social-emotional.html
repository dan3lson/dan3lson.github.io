Social/Emotional Objectives

We start with our Strategic Outcomes in mind and then determine what actions to complete to achieve our ambitious goals.

Tiered Systems of Support

Expected Outcomes
Students will report varied and healthier food selections in all SPS cafeterias as evidenced by responses to student/family surveys.

Actions
With families and the community, develop tiered systems of support and a response process that supports students’ social and emotional skills

Early Detection of Deficits

Expected Outcomes
Staff will be trained in the social emotional competencies and consistently implement SEL strategies as part of Tier I instruction

Actions
Develop and use core social emotional skills lists for early detection of skills deficits, continuing to assess and monitor progress

Professional Development

Expected Outcomes
A comprehensive Tier I SEL curriculum will be identified, implemented and information sessions held for parents/families in the curriculum

Actions
Provide professional development in the 5 areas of SEL competencies and the use of skills-based list and tiered supports

Well-being and Mental Health

Expected Outcomes
Tier II and Tier III interventions/supports will be identified and implemented consistently K-8

Actions
Partner with families and the community to support students’ well-being and mental health, providing education and information about both in school and out- of-school resources

Tiered Systems of Support

Expected Outcomes
The district will create a resource guide with local organization, contact information, specific available services, and accepted insurance that is provided to all administrators, nurses, psychologists, SAC, and counselors

Actions
Provide students with instruction based on the best practices of SEL; a strong focus on culture, integrated curriculum approaches, direct instruction, and community service learning, to enhance their SEL skill sets and protect against current and future mental health concerns.

Students’ Physical Health

Expected Outcomes
Decrease student-reported level of stress, lack of sleep, and risky behaviors of our secondary students

Actions
Promote students’ physical health by providing healthier food selections and appropriate recess and exercise
